Mind Australia

2020-2021:

Annual Report

Surmounting challenges and delivering new supports

In 2020-2021, we surmounted the challenges of the pandemic day after day, continuing to support our clients, carers and families, and strengthening and expanding our services around Australia.

Supporting our services through COVID

In 2020 there were no cases of COVID in our services.

In 2021 we supported a small number of residents who were COVID + with the more infectious DELTA variant in a handful of Victorian residential services.

Our staff did extraordinary work supporting clients through quarantine and minimising further spread. Mind supported furloughed and quarantined staff with extra leave arrangements.

Support and connection through lockdown

Telehealth options enabled us to provide connection and support to our clients when face to face services weren’t possible.

Mind is now also providing virtual support services nationally for Beyond Blue, for people experiencing mental distress related to the pandemic. A team of Mind counsellors, social workers psychologists and occupational therapists do intake assessments, provide counselling and refer people to other services where required.

Digital services

We are also using digital services to complement our existing services.

Mind is providing people with flexible support via video, phone and even text-chat in new Virtual Step Up, Step Down support services in Queensland and Western Australia. Funded by Queensland Health and Lottery West, these complement existing residential rehabilitation services, providing support in the crucial 28 days after a hospitalisation or a residential stay, to ease transition to life in the community.

Focusing on youth

Providing young people with the right, targeted recovery support when they are experiencing mental distress can make all the difference for their future outcomes.

Mind is delivering new youth outreach recovery support services in Latrobe Valley, Wodonga and Melbourne for the Department of Health and delivering psychosocial recovery support at Peninsula Health’s Frankston youth (and adult) Prevention and Recovery Care services.

Consolidating our partnerships

It means a lot to us when our partners renew our contracts to manage and deliver services. It reflects the quality of the support we have delivered to date and the great outcomes achieved by the clients we support.

Services renewed this Financial Year:

  • Psychological and psychosocial support to Bupa members following a private hospital mental health stay.
  • Aftercare suicide prevention support to LGBTIQ+ people in inner north and north west Melbourne delivered for North Western Melbourne Primary Health Network
  • Individual Recovery Support Program in Mackay and Cairns to 2023 for Queensland Health
  • Psychosocial recovery support at Bendigo Youth Prevention and Recovery Care service to 2023 for Bendigo Health
  • Individual psychosocial rehabilitation support services and mental health support services for SA Health in South Australia to 2024

Leading in peer-delivered services

Crisis Support Spaces are a peer-delivered and peer-led program Mind is piloting with Cairns and Mackay Hospital and Health Services to reduce Emergency Department presentations and inpatient admissions. Mind peer workers and a mental health clinician support recovery, in a more conducive setting, for people experiencing mental distress or suicidality.

Building our evidence base for better services

Implementation of our outcomes measurement program has expanded across our services so more clients can track their progress towards their goals and we and our partners can measure the impact of our recovery services.

My Better Life®

My Better Life® recovery planning program was singled out for commendation by Quality Innovation Performance (QIP) in our Quality Accreditation assessment this Financial Year. It is now a fundamental component of our service models, supporting clients to identify and achieve their recovery goals and giving us further measure of client satisfaction.

A place to call home

Mind subsidiary The Haven Foundation acquired sites to build multi-unit supported independent living residences in areas of high need in metro Melbourne and regional Victoria

  • Geelong: opened mid 2021
  • Laverton: opens late 2021
  • Mooroopna: opens late 2021
  • Pakenham: opens mid 2022
  • Epping opens mid 2022

Each Haven can accommodate up to 16 residents.

Measuring client outcomes

Mind clients can now track their recovery progress thanks to an app developed by Mind’s Research and Advocacy team and Digital Transformation team. This works by comparing a client’s self-assessment of various mental health and wellbeing indicators at the beginning and end of a support delivery. Mind is also using this data to achieve continual improvement across all its programs and services.

The Trajectories Report from Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander consultations

The final report in our landmark Trajectories research project, in partnership with the Australian Housing and Urban Research Institute, draws on consultations with 57 Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people in Alice Springs, Darwin, Melbourne and Port Hedland.

Revealing significant differences between Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander and mainstream language and understandings of mental illness, and of what it means to have a home or be homeless, the report highlights how these differences can undermine effective housing and mental health support for First Nations Australians, and outlines priorities for improvement

Looking ahead
